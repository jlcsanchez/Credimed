## 150-word description

Credimed is a healthcare-tech service that helps US dental PPO members recover out-of-network reimbursements for dental work performed by licensed providers in Mexico. The company files the insurance claim on the patient's behalf — translating Mexican procedure codes to the ADA CDT codes US insurers require, converting peso amounts to USD using the date-of-service exchange rate, and submitting the formatted claim to the insurer within 24 hours of payment.

The service is positioned for the estimated 1.4 million US dental tourists who travel to Mexico each year, half of whom pay out of pocket and never file for the partial reimbursement they're entitled to under their PPO plan. Credimed charges a flat $49 service fee with a money-back guarantee — patients pay nothing if the claim is eligible and Credimed cannot recover the reimbursement.

The company is US-incorporated, HIPAA-aligned, and based in Boston, Massachusetts.

## The story

In 2023, Juan Luis Sanchez watched a close friend battle the US dental insurance system after dental work in Tijuana, Mexico. The friend had had crowns and a root canal done — top-quality work at a fraction of the Boston price — and expected reimbursement from his PPO to be straightforward.

Six months later, after four rejected claims and one wrong CDT code (D2750 for porcelain-fused-to-metal crowns instead of D2740 for porcelain crowns), the friend had recovered only $1,200 of the $2,600 he was eligible to claim. A few conversations with other patients who had also traveled to Mexico for dental work revealed a pattern: almost everyone gives up on the reimbursement.

Roughly 1.4 million Americans travel to Mexico each year for dental procedures, drawn by prices that run 50–80% lower than US in-network rates. According to the American Dental Association, about half pay out of pocket and never file an out-of-network claim — leaving an estimated $400 million per year in eligible US PPO benefits unclaimed.

Credimed was built to capture that gap. The company files the claim correctly on the first attempt, including the CDT-code mapping, the FIX-rate currency conversion, the provider-RFC verification, and the format each insurer's claims system expects. Credimed is paid the same flat fee whether the recovered amount is $300 or $1,500, and pays the patient nothing — the reimbursement check goes directly from the insurer to the patient.

## What Credimed is not

- Not insurance. Credimed does not underwrite or sell insurance policies.
- Not a dental provider. Credimed does not offer medical or dental advice and does not refer patients to specific clinics.
- Not affiliated with any insurer. Credimed acts as the patient's limited claims representative under written authorization.
- Not commission-based. Credimed charges a flat fee per claim and does not take a percentage of the patient's recovered reimbursement.
