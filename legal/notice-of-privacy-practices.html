Notice of Privacy Practices (HIPAA)

Effective Date: April 27, 2026 · Last Revised: April 27, 2026 · Version 1.2

Draft template. This Notice is provided as a starting point and must be reviewed by qualified healthcare counsel before Credimed accepts paying customers. The exact wording and structure of an HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices is regulated under 45 C.F.R. § 164.520.
THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW YOUR PROTECTED HEALTH INFORMATION MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN GET ACCESS TO THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.

1. Our Role

Credimed, Inc. ("Credimed," "we," "us") provides claim preparation and submission services to assist patients in seeking reimbursement from their dental insurance carriers for out-of-network dental work performed in Mexico.

Credimed is not a healthcare clearinghouse, healthcare provider, health plan, or insurance company. We act as your authorized representative for the limited purpose of preparing and submitting your reimbursement claim, under written authorization in our Service Agreement. We use a third-party clearinghouse (currently Availity) to transmit prepared claims to insurance carriers.

When handling Protected Health Information ("PHI"), Credimed operates as a HIPAA Business Associate of any covered-entity insurer that requires a Business Associate Agreement, and otherwise as a service provider acting under your direct authorization. In either capacity, we comply with applicable provisions of the HIPAA Privacy Rule and Security Rule.

2. Our Legal Duties

Credimed is required by law to:

  • Maintain the privacy and security of your PHI;
  • Provide you with this Notice of our legal duties and privacy practices;
  • Follow the terms of this Notice currently in effect;
  • Notify you following a breach of unsecured PHI as required by law.

3. How We Use and Disclose PHI

We use and disclose PHI only as necessary to provide our Service or as permitted by law.

3.1 Claim Preparation and Submission

We use PHI to:

  • Prepare your insurance claim;
  • Submit it to your insurer through our clearinghouse vendor;
  • Respond to insurer requests for additional information.

3.2 Payment

We may use limited information to:

  • Process your payment;
  • Issue refunds.

PHI is not transmitted to payment processors.

3.3 Healthcare Operations

We may use PHI for:

  • Internal quality control;
  • Compliance and auditing;
  • System maintenance and security.

3.4 Uses and Disclosures Required or Permitted by Law

We may disclose PHI as permitted or required by HIPAA, including:

  • Public health activities;
  • Health oversight activities;
  • Judicial and administrative proceedings;
  • Law enforcement purposes;
  • To avert a serious threat to health or safety;
  • Specialized government functions (e.g., military, national security);
  • Workers' compensation;
  • Coroners, medical examiners, and funeral directors.

We do not currently disclose PHI for all of the above purposes but reserve the right to do so as permitted by law.

3.5 Authorization-Based Uses

We will use or disclose PHI for any purpose not described above only with your written authorization. You may revoke authorization at any time in writing.

4. Special Protections (HITECH Act)

Sale of PHI

HIPAA prohibits the sale of PHI without your explicit written authorization. We do not sell PHI.

Marketing

Use of PHI for marketing requires your explicit written authorization. We do not use PHI for marketing purposes.

5. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your PHI:

5.1 Right to Access

You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your PHI.

  • We will provide it in the format requested if readily producible;
  • Otherwise, we will provide a readable copy;
  • We respond within thirty (30) days, with one possible 30-day extension if necessary;
  • We may charge a reasonable, cost-based fee as permitted by law.

5.2 Right to Amendment

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete PHI.

5.3 Right to Restrict Use

You have the right to request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures of your PHI. We are not required to agree to your request, except that we must agree to restrict disclosures to a health plan when the disclosure is for the purpose of carrying out payment or healthcare operations and pertains solely to a healthcare item or service for which you have paid in full out of pocket (45 C.F.R. § 164.522(a)(1)(vi)).

5.4 Right to Confidential Communications

You may request that we communicate with you:

  • Via alternative methods;
  • Or at alternative locations.

We will accommodate reasonable requests.

5.5 Right to Accounting of Disclosures

You may request a list of disclosures of your PHI made for purposes other than:

  • Treatment;
  • Payment;
  • Healthcare operations.

The accounting covers up to six (6) years prior to your request.

The accounting does not include disclosures: (a) for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations; (b) to you or your personal representative; (c) for national security or intelligence purposes; (d) to correctional institutions or law enforcement officials regarding individuals in their custody; or (e) made prior to April 14, 2003.

5.6 Right to a Paper Copy

You have the right to receive a paper copy of this Notice at any time upon request.

6. How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise your rights, contact:

privacy@credimed.us

We will respond to:

  • Access requests within thirty (30) days, with one possible 30-day extension;
  • Amendment requests within sixty (60) days, with one possible 30-day extension;
  • Accounting requests within sixty (60) days, with one possible 30-day extension.

We will notify you in writing if an extension is needed and the reason.

7. Data Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards as required by the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C), including:

  • Encryption in transit and at rest;
  • Role-based access controls;
  • Audit logging;
  • Secure cloud infrastructure (Amazon Web Services, under an executed Business Associate Agreement covering all PHI processed in our environment).

8. Breach Notification

If your unsecured PHI is compromised, we will notify you:

  • Within sixty (60) days as required by HIPAA;
  • Sooner where required by applicable state law.

9. Changes to This Notice

We reserve the right to change this Notice. Material changes will be highlighted on the posted Notice for at least sixty (60) days. Any revised Notice applies to all PHI we maintain, regardless of when it was created or received.
